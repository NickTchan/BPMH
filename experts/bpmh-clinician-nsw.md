# Archetypal BPMH Practitioner — NSW Health Hospital Pharmacist

## Profile Summary

This profile represents the archetypal expert who routinely obtains and verifies Best Possible Medication Histories within NSW Health public hospitals. This profile is **inferred** from policy documents, professional standards, and published research — it requires validation through CTA interviews with actual practitioners.

## Credentials and Experience

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Hospital Pharmacist (typically Grade 2 or above) or Clinical Pharmacy Technician under pharmacist supervision |
| **Qualifications** | Bachelor of Pharmacy (or equivalent), AHPRA registration; additional credentialing in medication reconciliation preferred |
| **Experience** | 3+ years in hospital pharmacy with regular BPMH responsibility |
| **Typical caseload** | 8–15 BPMH interviews per shift, varying by ward acuity and patient complexity |
| **Context** | Works across multiple wards (medical, surgical, emergency) within an NSW public hospital |

## Key Competencies

- Systematic patient/carer interviewing using structured guides (CEC BPMH Interview Guide)
- Cross-referencing multiple medication information sources (patient interview, pharmacy records, GP letters, My Health Record, eMR)
- Identifying and classifying medication discrepancies (intentional vs. unintentional)
- Recognising high-risk medication classes and vulnerable patient populations
- Communicating findings to the medical team for prescribing decisions
- Documenting accurately in the eMR system

## Working Environment

- Time-pressured admission settings (ED, acute medical wards)
- Variable patient communication capacity (cognitive impairment, language barriers, acute illness)
- Multi-disciplinary team environment with handover requirements
- Access to eMR, My Health Record, and telephone contact with community pharmacies/GPs
