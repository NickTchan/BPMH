# Medication Adherence

## Definition

**Medication adherence** refers to the extent to which a patient's actual medication-taking behaviour matches the prescribed regimen. It encompasses whether the patient takes the medication, at the correct dose, at the correct frequency, and for the intended duration.

## Relevance to BPMH

The BPMH must capture what the patient is **actually taking**, not just what has been prescribed. The CEC BPMH Interview Guide and the Ho et al. (2026) review both emphasise probing for adherence patterns:
- *"Is there anything that makes taking your medications difficult?"*
- Documentation of adherence and changes in medication is a key requirement [REF: Ho et al., 2026]

A patient who has stopped taking a medication without informing their GP will have that medication listed in their pharmacy records but not in their actual use — this is a critical discrepancy that only a thorough BPMH interview can uncover.

## Application

Adherence assessment is embedded in the BPMH interview process ([PRC-001](../procedures/collect-medication-information.md)) and requires expert judgement to interpret ([DEC-003](../decisions/assess-adherence-pattern.md)).
