# Transfer of Care

## Definition

A **transfer of care** is any point at which responsibility for a patient's medication management passes from one clinician, team, or care setting to another. Medication reconciliation is required at every transfer of care.

## Types of Transfer of Care

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Admission** | Patient presents to hospital (ED, elective, or emergency admission) — BPMH obtained |
| **Intra-hospital transfer** | Patient moves between wards, units, or teams within the same facility |
| **Discharge** | Patient leaves hospital to return to community, aged care, or another facility |
| **Inter-facility transfer** | Patient is transferred between different health facilities |

## Why Transitions Are High-Risk

At each transfer, medication information must be accurately communicated. Breakdowns in communication are a primary cause of medication errors. The NSQHS Medication Safety Standard specifically requires reconciliation at all three transition points (admission, transfer, discharge).

## Application

Each transfer of care triggers the reconciliation process defined in **[TSK-002](../tasks/reconcile-medicines-transfer.md)**.
