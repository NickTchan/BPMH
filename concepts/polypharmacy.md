# Polypharmacy

## Definition

**Polypharmacy** refers to the concurrent use of multiple medications by a single patient. While there is no universally agreed threshold, it is commonly defined as the regular use of **5 or more medications** (sometimes termed "major polypharmacy" when ≥10 medications are used).

## Relevance to BPMH

Polypharmacy significantly increases the complexity and difficulty of obtaining an accurate BPMH:
- Patients may not remember all medications they are taking
- Multiple prescribers increase the likelihood of fragmented medication records
- Drug–drug interactions become more likely and harder to detect
- The risk of unintentional discrepancies increases proportionally with the number of medications

## Application

Polypharmacy is a key **complexity factor** ([CX-001](../complexity/polypharmacy-increases-bpmh-difficulty.md)) that affects the BPMH process. Patients with polypharmacy require more thorough verification and are at higher risk of medication discrepancies.
