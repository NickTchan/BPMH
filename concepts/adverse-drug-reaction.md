# Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR) and Drug Allergy

## Definition

An **Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR)** is any noxious, unintended, and undesired effect of a medication that occurs at doses used for prophylaxis, diagnosis, or treatment. A **drug allergy** is a specific type of ADR involving an immunological mechanism.

## Importance in BPMH

Allergies and adverse drug reactions must be documented as part of the BPMH [REF: NSW CEC]. The BPMH interview guide specifically asks: *"Do you have any allergies to medications or food?"* [REF: Ho et al., 2026].

## Distinction

| Term | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Drug allergy** | Immunologically-mediated reaction (e.g., IgE-mediated anaphylaxis, rash) | Penicillin causing hives or anaphylaxis |
| **Side effect / intolerance** | Non-immunological adverse effect (e.g., nausea, headache) | Codeine causing nausea |
| **Contraindication** | Clinical reason to avoid a medication (not an ADR) | Warfarin contraindicated in active bleeding |

## Application

Documented during the BPMH interview. The distinction between allergy and side effect is critical because mislabelled allergies lead to use of broader-spectrum, less effective, or more expensive alternative antibiotics.
