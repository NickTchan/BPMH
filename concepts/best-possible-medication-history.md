# Best Possible Medication History (BPMH)

## Definition

A **Best Possible Medication History (BPMH)** is an accurate and complete list of every medicine a patient is currently taking, including:
- Prescription medicines
- Over-the-counter (OTC) medicines
- Complementary medicines and natural health products
- Vitamins, minerals, and supplements

The BPMH must include for each medicine: **name, dose, frequency, route, and indication** where available. It must also document **allergies and adverse drug reactions**.

## Key Characteristics

- **Systematic:** Obtained through a structured process, not ad hoc questioning
- **Multi-source:** Derived using at least two sources of information, including a patient/carer interview where possible
- **Verified:** The accuracy of the information is confirmed against independent sources (pharmacy records, GP letters, My Health Record, etc.)
- **Current:** Reflects what the patient is *actually taking*, not just what has been prescribed

## Distinction from Simple Medication History

A simple medication history may be a single-source, unverified list (e.g., what the patient reports, or what the GP letter states). A BPMH is distinguished by the **verification step** — cross-referencing at least two sources to achieve the highest possible accuracy.

## Application

The BPMH is the foundation for all subsequent medication reconciliation activities:
- Used in **[PRC-003](../procedures/compare-bpmh-to-prescribed-medicines.md)** to compare against prescribed medicines
- Used in **[DEC-001](../decisions/classify-discrepancy-intentional-vs-unintentional.md)** to identify discrepancies
- Referenced at every transfer of care throughout the episode
