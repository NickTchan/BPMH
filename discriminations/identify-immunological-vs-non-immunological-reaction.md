# Identify Immunological vs. Non-Immunological Reaction from Patient Narrative

## Description

The expert's ability to listen to a patient's description of a medication reaction and classify it as immunological (true allergy) or non-immunological (side effect/intolerance) based on the specific language and symptoms the patient uses.

## Perceptual Cue

The patient's verbal description of their reaction:
- **Immunological cues:** "swelling," "couldn't breathe," "hives," "rash all over," "went to hospital," "lips swelled up"
- **Non-immunological cues:** "upset my stomach," "made me feel sick," "gave me a headache," "made me drowsy," "diarrhoea"

## Threshold

- Any description of **airway involvement, swelling, or widespread rash** → classify as true allergy
- Any description of **gastrointestinal upset, drowsiness, or headache** → classify as side effect/intolerance
- **Vague or uncertain descriptions** → document as "allergy — reaction unknown" and flag for clarification

## What Novices Miss

Novices may accept any patient statement of "allergy" at face value without probing for the reaction details, leading to false allergy labels that persist in the medical record and affect future prescribing.
