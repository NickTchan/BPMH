# Structured Patient Introduction for BPMH

## Purpose

To establish rapport and set expectations at the start of a BPMH interview using a standardised framework that reduces patient anxiety and improves information quality.

## HAIDET Framework

The HAIDET framework provides a structured approach to the opening moments of the BPMH interview:

| Step | Action | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **H** — Handshake | Greet the patient and caregiver warmly; introduce yourself and your role | *"Hi, I'm [name], the pharmacist. I'm here to go through [patient's] medications with you."* |
| **A** — Ask patient preference | Ask who the patient would like involved in the conversation | *"Who would you like to be part of this discussion?"* |
| **I** — Introduce | Explain what the BPMH is and why it matters | *"I'm going to ask about all the medicines [patient] takes — including ones from the pharmacy, over-the-counter products, and vitamins. This helps us make sure nothing is missed."* |
| **D** — Duration | Give an estimate of how long the interview will take | *"This usually takes about 10–15 minutes."* |
| **E** — Explain | Explain the process and what you'll be asking about | *"I'll start by asking about [patient's] health background, then go through each medication. I may also ask about any allergies or reactions."* |
| **T** — Thank | Thank the patient and caregiver for their time and cooperation | *"Thank you for helping me with this — it really helps us keep [patient] safe."* |

## Application

Use this framework at the start of every BPMH interview ([PRC-001](../procedures/collect-medication-information.md)). The structure is especially important in paediatric settings where parents may be anxious and children may be frightened.

## Rationale

A structured introduction reduces uncertainty, builds trust, and signals to the patient and caregiver that the clinician is organised and thorough. This directly improves the quality of information obtained in subsequent questioning.
