# Family Illness Context Screening

## Purpose

To systematically screen for household and community illness exposures that may explain the patient's presentation and inform medication history accuracy in paediatric settings.

## Why This Matters

In paediatric BPMH, the patient's symptoms and medication use are often linked to illnesses circulating in their household, sibling group, or childcare setting. A child presenting with fever and cough may have been exposed at preschool; a sibling with similar symptoms may have already received antibiotics that the parent has not yet reported.

## Screening Questions

During the background review phase of the BPMH interview ([PRC-001](../procedures/collect-medication-information.md)), include:

1. **Sibling symptoms:** *"Has anyone else at home been unwell recently?"*
2. **Childcare/school exposure:** *"Are there other children at [patient's] preschool or school who have been sick?"*
3. **Recent household illnesses:** *"Has anyone in the household had a cold, fever, or stomach bug in the last week or two?"*
4. **Shared medications:** *"Has anyone else's medication been given to [patient]?"* (e.g., sibling's paracetamol)

## Clinical Relevance

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Sibling with similar symptoms + recent antibiotic | Patient may have received shared doses; document as OTC use |
| Multiple household members unwell | Viral illness likely; may explain presentation and medication changes |
| Childcare outbreak reported | Increases likelihood of infectious aetiology; may affect medication decisions |
| Parent gave sibling's medication | Dose may be inappropriate for patient's weight/age; flag for review |

> **Tacit cue:** Parents often don't consider a sibling's illness as relevant to the patient's medication history. The pharmacist should ask proactively — this information frequently explains medication changes or omissions that would otherwise appear as unexplained discrepancies.
