# BPMH Skill Progression Stages

## Stage 1: Novice (0–6 months / <50 supervised BPMHs)

**Characteristics:**
- Relies entirely on structured guides and checklists
- Accepts patient self-report at face value
- Does not probe beyond the guide's questions
- Struggles to reconcile conflicting sources
- Documents medication names but may omit dose, frequency, or indication
- Does not recognise high-risk medication patterns

**Common Errors:**
- Missing OTC and complementary medications
- Failing to distinguish allergy from side effect
- Not verifying against any source beyond the patient interview
- Incomplete documentation

## Stage 2: Competent (6–24 months / 50–200 BPMHs)

**Characteristics:**
- Uses the guide fluently but begins to adapt questions to the patient
- Routinely verifies against at least one additional source
- Begins to recognise common discrepancy patterns
- Documents more completely
- Starts to identify high-risk medications

**Transition Trigger:** Repeated exposure to discrepancy cases and feedback from senior pharmacists.

## Stage 3: Proficient (2–5 years / 200+ BPMHs)

**Characteristics:**
- Conducts the interview as a diagnostic conversation, not a checklist
- Uses clinical knowledge to anticipate what medications the patient should be on
- Rapidly identifies and triages discrepancies
- Knows which sources to prioritise in different contexts
- Mentors novices

## Stage 4: Expert (5+ years / extensive caseload across multiple contexts)

**Characteristics:**
- Intuitive pattern recognition for medication discrepancies
- Mental simulation of the patient's medication ecosystem
- Adapts approach seamlessly to patient complexity, acuity, and communication capacity
- Contributes to local policy and training
