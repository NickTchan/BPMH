# Immunisation Status Relevance to BPMH

## Context

This decision occurs during the **background review** phase of the BPMH interview, before current medication collection. Immunisation status is often the first topic raised in a paediatric BPMH.

## Cue

The clinician is conducting the initial interview and has established rapport with the parent/caregiver.

## Threshold

The clinician must determine: **Is the patient's immunisation status relevant to their current presentation and medication history?**

## Why Immunisation Status Matters

- **Vaccine-preventable illnesses:** An unimmunised or partially immunised child presenting with fever and rash raises different differential diagnoses than a fully immunised child
- **Recent immunisations:** A child who received a vaccine in the last 48-72 hours may have fever, irritability, or local reactions that mimic illness or affect medication administration
- **Medication interactions:** Some immunisations interact with medications (e.g., immunosuppressants, anticoagulants)
- **Documentation completeness:** Immunisation history is part of the complete medication record

## Assessment Questions

1. *"Is [patient] up to date with their immunisations?"*
2. *"Have they had any vaccines recently - in the last week or two?"*
3. *"Do you have their immunisation record with you?"* (if available, review it)

## Action Table

| Finding | Implication | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Fully immunised, no recent vaccines | Standard risk profile | Proceed with medication history |
| Recent vaccine (within 72 hours) | May explain fever, irritability, local reactions | Document vaccine date and type; consider when assessing current symptoms |
| Incomplete or unknown immunisation status | Increased risk of vaccine-preventable illness | Flag for medical team review; document as "immunisation status unknown" |
| Parent reports adverse reaction to previous vaccine | May affect future immunisation decisions | Document reaction details; flag for medical team |

> **Tacit cue:** Immunisation status is often the easiest entry point for a paediatric BPMH interview. Parents are usually well-informed about their child's vaccination schedule, and asking about it first builds confidence before moving to more complex medication questions.
