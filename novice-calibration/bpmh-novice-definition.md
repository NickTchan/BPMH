# Novice Definition — BPMH Practitioner

> **⚠️ This is an operational placeholder definition. It requires expert validation via CTA interview.**

## Operational Definition

A **novice** in the BPMH domain is a clinician (pharmacist, nurse, or medical officer) who:

1. **Has completed theoretical training** on medication reconciliation and the BPMH process but has limited practical experience (< 6 months or < 50 supervised BPMH interviews).
2. **Relies heavily on checklists** and structured guides without yet developing the perceptual ability to detect when a patient's narrative suggests missing or inaccurate information.
3. **Accepts patient self-report at face value** without systematically probing for OTC products, complementary medicines, adherence patterns, or recent changes.
4. **Struggles to reconcile conflicting sources** — when the patient's report differs from the GP letter or pharmacy record, the novice may not know which source to prioritise or how to resolve the discrepancy.
5. **Misses high-risk patterns** — does not readily identify polypharmacy red flags, look-alike/sound-alike medication confusions, or the clinical significance of omitted chronic medications.
6. **Documents incompletely** — may record medication names but omit dose, frequency, route, indication, or last-dose timing.

## What the Expert Does Differently

The expert, by contrast, uses the interview as a **diagnostic conversation** — listening for cues that suggest unreported medications, probing strategically based on the patient's condition, and mentally simulating what medications the patient *should* be on based on their diagnoses. The expert also knows when a BPMH is "good enough" to proceed and when further verification is warranted.
