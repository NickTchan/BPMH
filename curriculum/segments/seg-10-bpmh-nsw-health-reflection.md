## Rationale

This segment provides cognitive feedback — the type of feedback that stimulates learners to compare their own mental models with expert models. It is placed at the end of the curriculum (after the full-performance task in Segment 07) so that learners have sufficient experience to reflect meaningfully on the differences between novice and expert approaches. This is a Tier 3 segment because the goal is learner reflection on mental model alignment, not performance assessment.

## Content Overview

This segment presents three expert–novice contrasts drawn from the learning curve observations [LC-001] and novice calibration [NOV-001]. First, the interview approach: novices follow a checklist rigidly and accept patient self-report at face value, while experts conduct a diagnostic conversation, anticipating missing medications and probing strategically [MM-001]. Second, the discrepancy approach: novices treat all discrepancies equally, requiring prescriber confirmation for each one, while experts rapidly triage discrepancies using clinical context [DISC-001]. Third, the verification approach: novices apply the same verification approach regardless of patient context, while experts adapt source selection to complexity and time pressure [DEC-004]. For each contrast, the learner is presented with a scenario, asked to reflect on their own approach, and then shown the expert's approach with an explanation of *why* it works. The segment also addresses the flagged gaps around the expert mental model [GAP-005] and novice-to-competent transition [GAP-007], acknowledging where tacit knowledge is still being captured.

### What the learner encounters

- Expert–novice contrast: interview approach (checklist vs. diagnostic conversation) [MM-001, LC-001]
- Expert–novice contrast: discrepancy approach (equal treatment vs. rapid triage) [DISC-001, LC-001]
- Expert–novice contrast: verification approach (one-size-fits-all vs. context-adaptive) [DEC-004, LC-001]
- Structured reflection prompts for each contrast
- Acknowledgement of flagged gaps [GAP-005, GAP-007]

### What the learner produces or performs

- Written reflection on how their approach compares to the expert model for each contrast
- Self-assessment of which skill dimensions (process, soft skills, clinical) they feel most confident in [LC-001]

## LTEM Justification

This segment targets Tier 3 (Learner Perceptions) because the goal is learner reflection on mental model alignment and self-assessment of skill development. The segment does not assess decision-making or task performance — it collects learner perceptions about the relevance and realism of the expert models presented.

## Implementation Notes

- Use open-ended reflection prompts rather than multiple-choice questions.
- The segment should feel like a debrief, not an assessment — encourage honest self-reflection.
- Consider making this segment optional for experienced practitioners who may not need the mental model comparison.
- The flagged gaps [GAP-005, GAP-007] should be presented transparently: "We know experts think differently, but we're still capturing exactly how. Here's what we know so far."
