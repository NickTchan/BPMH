# BPMH NSW Health Curriculum Overview

## Executive Summary

This curriculum addresses the complex cognitive skill of obtaining a Best Possible Medication History (BPMH) within the NSW Health public hospital system. BPMH is the foundational step in medication reconciliation — a formal four-step process mandated by NSW Health policy (PD2022_032) and the NSQHS Medication Safety Standard [CON-001, CON-002]. The stakes are high: unintentional medication discrepancies affect >50% of hospitalised patients and are linked to adverse drug events, readmission, and mortality [RISK-001, RISK-002].

This is not a simple procedural training need. The BPMH practitioner must integrate multiple non-routine skills — conducting diagnostic patient interviews, selecting and prioritising verification sources, resolving conflicting information, classifying discrepancies, and adapting to patient complexity — alongside routine procedural skills such as documentation and comparison. The domain is characterised by three major complexity factors: polypharmacy (≥5 medications) [CX-001], patient communication barriers [CX-002], and time pressure in acute settings [CX-003]. Expert practitioners develop pattern recognition and mental models over years of practice [LC-001], making this a textbook case for 4C/ID whole-task, competency-based design.

The curriculum covers the full medication reconciliation cycle: obtaining BPMH at admission [TSK-001], reconciling medicines at transfer of care [TSK-002], and supplying accurate medicines information at discharge [TSK-003]. It is designed for eLearning delivery only, with all four 4C/ID components (learning tasks, supportive information, procedural information, and part-task practice) adapted for asynchronous delivery.
