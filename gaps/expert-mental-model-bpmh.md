# Expert Mental Model of the Patient's Medication Ecosystem

## Gap Description

The expert's internal model of how they construct a mental picture of the patient's complete medication ecosystem — combining knowledge of the patient's diagnoses, expected medication classes, reported medications, and verification sources — is referenced implicitly but never articulated.

## Why This Matters

This mental model is the foundation of the expert's ability to conduct the BPMH interview as a **diagnostic conversation** rather than a checklist exercise. It enables the expert to anticipate missing medications, probe strategically, and detect inconsistencies. Without understanding this model, training cannot teach novices to think like experts.

## What We Know

- Experts use clinical knowledge to anticipate what medications a patient should be on
- Experts adapt their questioning based on the patient's responses

## What We Don't Know

- The structure of the expert's mental model (what categories, relationships, and expectations it contains)
- How the expert updates the model as new information emerges during the interview
- How the expert handles uncertainty within the model
- Whether the model differs for different patient populations (e.g., elderly vs. young, psychiatric vs. medical)

## CTA Priority: 5

This is core tacit knowledge without which the domain cannot be taught effectively. It is the fundamental cognitive structure that distinguishes expert from novice BPMH practice.
