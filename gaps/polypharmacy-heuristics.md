# Expert Heuristics for Managing Polypharmacy in BPMH

## Gap Description

While polypharmacy is recognised as a complexity factor, the **specific heuristics** experts use to manage polypharmacy patients during BPMH are not documented beyond the general principle of "use more sources."

## Why This Matters

Polypharmacy patients are the highest-risk group for medication discrepancies. Understanding the expert's shortcuts and pattern-recognition rules would enable more effective training.

## What We Know

- Polypharmacy increases discrepancy risk
- Experts use more verification sources for polypharmacy patients

## What We Don't Know

- The expert's prioritisation rules (which medications to verify first in a 15-medication list)
- The expert's "good enough" threshold for polypharmacy verification
- Whether experts use medication class grouping strategies
- How experts handle medications they are unfamiliar with in a polypharmacy list

## CTA Priority: 3

Useful context that would improve instruction but is not as safety-critical as the core decision points.
