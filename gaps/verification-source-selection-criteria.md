# Expert Criteria for Prioritising Verification Sources

## Gap Description

Authoritative sources list the available verification sources for BPMH (pharmacy records, GP letters, My Health Record, patient medication lists, etc.) but do not articulate **how the expert decides which source to prioritise** in different clinical contexts. The expert likely has a mental hierarchy of source reliability and a context-dependent selection strategy that is not documented.

## Why This Matters

In time-pressured settings (ED), the expert must quickly choose the most reliable accessible source. In complex cases (polypharmacy, cognitive impairment), the expert must know which combination of sources provides the highest confidence. Without understanding this decision framework, training cannot teach novices how to make these choices effectively.

## What We Know

- At least two sources are required, one being the patient/carer interview [REF: Ho et al., 2026]
- Pharmacy dispensing records are generally considered highly reliable
- My Health Record may be outdated

## What We Don't Know

- The expert's internal ranking of source reliability
- How the expert weighs recency vs. completeness vs. accessibility
- Whether the expert uses different source hierarchies for different patient populations (e.g., aged care vs. community-dwelling)
- How the expert decides when "enough" verification has been achieved

## CTA Priority: 4

This gap affects multiple procedures and decisions. It is a significant tacit knowledge area that novices universally struggle with.
