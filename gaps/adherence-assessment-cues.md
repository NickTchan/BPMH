# Expert Cues and Probing Strategies for Detecting Non-Adherence

## Gap Description

Sources emphasise the importance of documenting actual medication use (not just prescribed use) but do not articulate the **specific cues** experts use to detect non-adherence or the **probing strategies** they employ to elicit accurate information without making patients defensive.

## Why This Matters

Non-adherence is common and has significant implications for the BPMH. If the expert records the prescribed dose rather than the actual dose, the entire reconciliation process is based on incorrect information.

## What We Know

- The BPMH interview guide asks: *"Is there anything that makes taking your medications difficult?"*
- Adherence documentation is a requirement

## What We Don't Know

- The verbal cues (hesitation, vagueness, contradictions) that signal non-adherence
- The behavioural cues (avoiding eye contact, changing subject) that signal non-adherence
- The specific probing questions experts use beyond the standard guide
- How experts create a non-judgemental environment that encourages honest disclosure
- How experts document non-adherence patterns in a way that is clinically useful

## CTA Priority: 4

This is a core tacit knowledge area that directly affects the accuracy of the BPMH.
