# Transition Triggers from Novice to Competent BPMH Practitioner

## Gap Description

No source describes **what moves a practitioner from novice to competent** in BPMH practice. The learning curve stages in [LC-001](../learningcurve/bpmh-skill-progression.md) are inferred from general skill acquisition theory, not from domain-specific evidence.

## Why This Matters

Understanding the transition triggers would enable targeted training interventions and more accurate competency assessment.

## What We Know (Expert Observations)

Expert practitioners have identified the following transition challenges and observations:

- **Processes are learnable via SOPs; soft skills require on-the-job experience** — Following a checklist or documentation tool is straightforward. Adapting communication style to different patients and prescribers is not.
- **Common novice failure mode: poor patient approach causing defensiveness** — Novices may put patients on the defensive through their interviewing approach, blocking the flow of accurate medication information.
- **Prescriber communication requires reading individual preferences** — Some doctors prefer email communication, others prefer in-person conversation. Novices must learn to adapt to each prescriber's style, which is highly situational and cannot be taught through SOPs.
- **Post-registration independence means no safety net for detecting errors** — Registered pharmacists work independently after registration. Unlike interns, there is no shoulder-checking, so errors may go undetected.
- **Clinical knowledge builds via accumulated patient case studies** — Each patient encounter contributes to the practitioner's clinical knowledge base. This is an iterative process that cannot be accelerated.
- **Expert's own path:** Approximately 4–5 years of clinical experience before first exposure to a formal medication reconciliation documentation tool, suggesting that BPMH-specific skills build on a broader clinical foundation.

## What We Don't Know

- Whether there is a minimum number of BPMHs required for the transition
- Whether formal supervision or self-directed practice is more effective
- What observable behaviours reliably indicate the transition has occurred

Important for curriculum design but not safety-critical.
