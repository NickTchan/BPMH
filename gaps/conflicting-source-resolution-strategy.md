# Expert Strategy for Resolving Conflicting Medication Sources

## Gap Description

Sources acknowledge that conflicting information between verification sources is common but do not articulate the **expert's decision framework** for determining which source to trust when they disagree. This is a safety-critical tacit knowledge area.

## Why This Matters

When the patient says they take 10mg of Medication X, the pharmacy record shows 5mg, and the GP letter says it was ceased — the expert must make a judgement call about what the patient is actually taking. An incorrect decision can lead to under-dosing, over-dosing, or inappropriate cessation.

## What We Know

- Conflicts between sources are common
- The BPMH must reflect what the patient is *actually taking*
- Contacting the pharmacy or GP directly is an option

## What We Don't Know

- The expert's hierarchy of source credibility (e.g., is a dispensing record always more reliable than a prescription record?)
- How the expert assesses patient reliability as a source
- The expert's threshold for "I need to call the pharmacy" vs. "I'm confident enough"
- How the expert handles conflicts between two professional sources (e.g., GP letter vs. specialist letter)

## CTA Priority: 4

This is a safety-critical decision point with no articulated criteria in the literature.
