# Obtain Best Possible Medication History at Admission

## Task Description

The end-to-end task of obtaining a Best Possible Medication History (BPMH) for a patient presenting to a NSW public health facility. This is the first step in the medication reconciliation process and must be completed on presentation or as early as possible in the episode of care.

## Task Boundary

**Starts when:** A patient presents to a NSW public health facility (ED, ward admission, or scheduled admission) and requires medication management.

**Ends when:** A verified, documented BPMH is recorded in the patient's clinical record and available to the treating team for prescribing decisions.

## Constituent Procedures

This whole task comprises four sequential procedures (per the NSW CEC and WHO High5s framework):

1. **[PRC-001](../procedures/collect-medication-information.md)** — Collect information to compile a list of the patient's current medicines
2. **[PRC-002](../procedures/verify-medication-history-accuracy.md)** — Confirm the accuracy of that list to achieve a BPMH
3. **[PRC-003](../procedures/compare-bpmh-to-prescribed-medicines.md)** — Compare the BPMH against prescribed medicines at every transfer of care
4. **[PRC-004](../procedures/supply-medicines-information.md)** — Supply accurate medicines information to the patient and next care provider

## Stakeholders

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Medical Officer / Nurse Practitioner | May initiate medication history collection; ultimately responsible for prescribing decisions |
| Pharmacist | Leads BPMH verification; identifies and resolves discrepancies |
| Registered Nurse | May collect initial medication history; administers medications per chart |
| Patient / Carer | Primary source of medication use information |
| Community Pharmacy / GP | Verification sources for prescribed and dispensed medications |

## Performance Standards

- BPMH must be obtained on presentation or as early as possible in the episode of care [REF: NSW Health PD2022_032]
- At least two sources of information must be used, including patient/carer interview where possible [REF: Ho et al., 2026]
- All medicine classes must be captured: prescription, OTC, complementary, natural health products [REF: NSW CEC]
- Allergies and adverse drug reactions must be documented [REF: NSW CEC]
